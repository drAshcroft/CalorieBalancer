    Journal

    How do I log my weight?

        Click the journal button    to navigate to the journal dialog.  Here you can record all your progress.  To log your weight, enter it into the field Weight.  The program asks for your morning weight.  This is because the morning weight is usually the the most stable time to measure your weight.  You can enter a weight from any time of the day, if you like, just try to be consistent about the time of the day as your weight can fluctuate over 3-5 pounds in a day.

    How do I log my body fat percentage?

       Click the journal button    to navigate to the journal dialog. You can log your body fat percentage in a number of ways.  The best is to measure your body's dimensions at a set period of time, such as every week.  The program will then calculate your body fat percentage.  The other method is to use a body fat percentage scale to get this value.  Since body fat is actually the most important number to track in a diet, this should measured frequently.

    How do I log my energy, hunger and stress?

        Click the journal button    to navigate to the journal dialog.  The program also asks you to log other items.  You can indicated your hunger, energy, and stress by clicking a value from 1-10.  10 Being the most hungry you have ever been.  These numbers help determine your level of discomfort from the diet.  This can be very important in determining how many calories you wish to go under each day.

    I want to record other diet items? (weight lifting, strength, soreness...)

        It is possible to track many things that are not listed on the Journal page right now.  You will need to email me at Support@CalorieBalanceDiet.com and if the work is not to rigerous, I will be glad to design a new Journal page for you.

    How do I record my diet experiences?

       Click the journal button    to navigate to the journal dialog.  At the bottom of the Journal, there is a space for you to journal your experiences with the diet.  This is a private space to deal with your emotions and to track your mental and emotional dieting problems and successes.  Research has shown that keeping a journal can improve the dieting experience.

    How do I see a record of my weight progress?

        After bringing up the Journal, you can either Click the Progress report link on the Journal Webpage, or you can click Reports at the top of the form and then Progress Report.

    How can I get a log of my foods eaten?

        After bringing up the Journal, you can click the Food log link or on the Journal Webpage, you can click Food log.  You will be asked how many weeks you wish to track and then the program will show your average nutrition over that period as well as all the foods that have been eaten.

    Can I see today's nutrition information?

        After bring up the Journal, click the Today's Nutrition link.  The nutitrition information will be shown.

    What is my metabolism?

        The program will attempt to analyse your metabolism.  This requires a large number of weights to be recorded as well as having all your calories recorded in between.  If this has been done, then the program will be able to determine how many calories it takes for you to lose a pound, as well as the BMR.  To get to the metabolism report, click the Journal Button and then click Metabolism Report.

    What macronutrient (Protein, Fat, Sugar, Carbs, Fiber) helps me diet?

        With the same requirements as above, the program is able to determine if the percent of a certain nutrient is helpful for your diet success.  The program compares the amount of calories it takes to lose weight against the amount of Fat, Sugar, Carbs, and Fiber in the diet.  To see this report, click on the Journal button and then click Nutrient Report.
