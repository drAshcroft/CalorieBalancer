The Exercise Tracker

The exercise tracker makes it possible to succeed in your workout program.  Learn how many calories you are burning, how much more you get to eat when you exercise, and make sure that you are progressing as needed.  The exercise tracker contains 400 activities that you will enjoy, so explore. 

 How do I enter an exercise?

Click on the exercise button to the left of the page.  This will take you to the exercise tracker.  As you look across the top of the page, you will see that a whole week is shown on this page.  This allows you to quickly and easily plan out your workouts.  To the left of the page you can enter the exercise names.

           

Click the space under Exercise.  Type in “Walking”.  You will see many choices, but chose “Walking(steps)”.  ‘steps’ refers to how any steps you have taken in that day(for those who like pedometers).   If you do not count your steps, chose “Walking (Speed/Minutes)"  This allows you to put your speed and time in to the program.   The paranthesis tell you what the program is expecting.  Most of the activities require the minutes you have exercised.  This is shown in the picture above.

Next you must enter your numbers.  Click on the appropriate day and row.  In the first example, the number of steps has been entered.   In the second, the speed  in miles per hour and minutes have been entered.  And last, the number of minutes were added

 

How do I make a new exercise?

  While in the exercise tracker, a new button appears to the left of the page in the toolbar.  Click the new exercise button and a small window will pop up.  Type in your Exercise Name.  You will be able to see the other options, so look if there is something similar.
  You will have three options after you enter the name 

  1. If you have a formula for the number of calories burned, you can directly enter this into the program.  

Some websites will give a number for calories burned per minute per pound.  This is entered in as

0.06*weight*minutes.  This is a very advanced feature that should only be used if you have this information or wish to make a more complicated calorie response..

  1.   If you just have a number of calories burned in an hour.

Many websites and books will just list one number for the calories burned in an hour of exercising.  This is usually for a 150 pound man.  The program will make a formula from this number when you enter it into the program.  

  1.   Last, use the Looks Like option if there is already a similar exercise in the database.  Click this option, then a second dropdown box will appear.  Choose the closest activity and the entry will be updated.

 How do you make the squares black or gray? What does that mean?

Blacking the Box out.
We give you the option to block out certain parts of the week.  This can help you visually remember which days you should work out.  Simply put a “–“ in the boxes you wish to black out.  You can remove the black by clicking on the box and removing the “-“. 
Graying the box
Gray boxes are not counted in the calories below to help you plan your work out.  Just entering a value into these boxes will remove the gray.  Use the “*” to gray a box.
