Use the Internet to find new foods

  There are times when you cannot find the desired food in the database.  You can use the internet to find a new food.  The program has a built in utility to make adding new foods from the internet easier.  To use this tool, either click New Food, and then the Search Internet button.  Or if you are searching for a food in the calorie counter, select Big Search.  The internet food dialog will appear.

 

The dialog allows you to search from any internet page in the browser to the left.  You can enter the address of your favorite search engine in the address page.  I have found that Yahoo gives very good results with the phrase, "Calories in XXX food"  Where XXX is the desired food.   The program will constantly try to read the webpages to find the nutritional information. 

Once you have found the desired nutritional information webpage, shown here is the USDA page, the program will try to read the page to fill in the information.  You will need to make sure that the information is correct, enter the foodname, and usually the serving size.  If you do not know how many grams are in a serving, just guess or enter 100.  Click Save food to finish.

 

You can search the big database on our website by clicking Calorie Balance Webpage at the top.  This will operate the same way, only from the our database.  Last you can look for a similar food from the database and save a new food by comparision.
